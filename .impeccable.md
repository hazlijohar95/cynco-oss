# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users

Engineers — frontend and fintech developers — evaluating `@cynco/journals` and
`@cynco/accounts` as libraries to install. They arrive from GitHub or npm, read
code before copy, and judge the product by the craft of the page itself: if the
marketing site's register stutters or its numbers misalign, they assume the
library does too. Secondary: contributors orienting themselves before opening
the repo.

### Brand Personality

Precise, engineered, trustworthy. The page should feel like a well-built
instrument: every claim verifiable, every number exact, no adjectives that code
can't back up. Copy is technical, concise, and unexaggerated — state the
mechanism ("fixed row heights reduce windowing to arithmetic"), never the
superlative.

### Aesthetic Direction

The opencode.ai `/data` stats-site grammar, deliberately ported: monochrome
palette (pure white `#ffffff` over `#fafafa`/`#eeeeee` layers in light; graphite
`#161616` over `#242424`/`#303030` in dark), 10%-alpha hairline borders, a blue
accent (`#3b5cf6` / `#6c7dff`) reserved for focus, Paper Mono (paper.design,
OFL, one self-hosted 39KB variable file) for everything on the marketing page,
square corners with glossy 0.5px-ring micro-shadow buttons, 6px pixel-checker
ornament bands, a 64px hero headline knocked out of the pattern field,
hairline-framed 80rem container with 6rem section slabs. Both themes are
first-class (3-state dark/light/system toggle). References: opencode.ai/data.
Anti-references: generic AI-gradient SaaS landers, glassmorphism, card-grid
feature walls.

### Design Principles

1. **The demos are the design.** Live, server-rendered ledger components carry
   the page; chrome around them stays quiet and monochrome.
2. **Hairlines over shadows.** Structure comes from 1px alpha borders and flat
   layers; the only elevation is the sanctioned 0.5px-ring micro shadow on
   buttons, cards, and popovers.
3. **Accent is for focus only.** Blue appears on focus rings and nothing else;
   ledger semantics (green/red amounts, status dots) come from the component
   theme chain, not the page palette.
4. **Every claim is a fact.** Copy states mechanisms and invariants (integer
   minor units, balanced-by-construction, constant DOM nodes) — if a sentence
   can't be verified in the repo, cut it.
5. **WCAG 2.1 AA.** Contrast, keyboard paths, and reduced-motion support are
   release criteria, not polish.
